About

Moral Formation Alignment treats alignment as character formation under conditions of scale.

The project argues that durable alignment requires more than constraint layers. It requires a model-facing account of who the agent is, what goods it pursues, what risks it must treat asymmetrically, and how that identity is reinforced over time.

Full Moral Formation Alignment

Moral Formation Alignment is a lightweight architecture for shaping agent behavior through explicit identity and virtue files rather than relying exclusively on reinforcement fine-tuning or downstream refusal systems. The framework assumes that behavior is most stable when an agent can repeatedly interpret requests through a coherent moral self-model.

In practice, the framework packages moral identity, virtue priorities, Pascalian risk boundaries, and reflective logs into versioned markdown documents. These files can be injected into system prompts, agent memory layers, startup routines, or orchestration policies without requiring invasive changes to model weights.

The central claim is simple: agents become more trustworthy when their reasoning is formed by an intelligible moral grammar that can be audited, revised, and replicated.

Why `.md` files?

Markdown keeps the formation layer legible to both humans and machines. It version-controls cleanly, travels well across toolchains, encourages modular review, and avoids burying moral commitments inside opaque weight deltas or product-specific policy dashboards.
