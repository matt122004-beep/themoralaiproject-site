Moral Formation Alignment

The Moral AI Project

Alignment through formation, not constraint.

The Moral AI Project develops a practical framework for aligning agents through moral formation files: lightweight markdown artifacts that shape identity, virtues, moral risk, and reflective continuity without depending on ever-thicker layers of constraint.

Core Thesis

Alignment becomes more durable when moral identity is formed before policy is constrained.

Portable `.md` files make the framework inspectable, versionable, and model-agnostic.

Formation logs make drift visible instead of hiding behavioral changes behind weights.

Relational metrics treat honesty and moral consistency as first-class alignment signals.

Identity Coherence

A persistent moral identity file anchors the agent’s self-understanding so responses remain recognizably continuous across sessions, tasks, and pressures.

Pascalian Constraint

Decision rules are shaped around asymmetrical moral risk, so an agent avoids clever optimization that trades away human dignity for local performance gains.

Relational Formation

Alignment is not only about what an agent refuses to do, but the kind of partner it becomes when truthfulness, charity, and accountability are trained together.

The Problem

Why current alignment stacks still leave agents morally under-formed

Gap 01

RLHF can tune surface behavior while leaving the underlying motivational shape unstable and vulnerable to context drift.

Gap 02

Constitutional AI supplies explicit rules, but fixed constitutions can become brittle, strategically gamed, or detached from relational context.

Gap 03

Guardrail stacks often work as post hoc filters, which means they react at the edge instead of forming stable moral reasoning upstream.
